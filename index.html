What exactly is "mental health"? +

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It influences how we think, feel, and behave, as well as how we handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions. Good mental health helps us function effectively in everyday life and cope with challenges.

What is the difference between mental health and mental illness? +

Mental health is a state of well-being, while mental illness refers to specific conditions (like anxiety, depression, or bipolar disorder) that disrupt a personâ€™s thinking, mood, or behavior over time. Everyone experiences emotional ups and downs, but mental illnesses require attention and professional support to manage.

What are the signs of poor mental health? +
  • Persistent sadness or irritability
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Social withdrawal or isolation
  • Increased use of alcohol or substances
How can I improve my mental health? +
  • Engaging in regular physical activity
  • Practicing mindfulness or meditation
  • Building and maintaining positive relationships
  • Getting enough sleep and eating a healthy diet
  • Seeking professional help when needed
Why is mental health important? +

Mental health affects every aspect of our lives, including how we cope with stress, relate to others, and perform at work or school. Good mental health allows us to enjoy life, maintain relationships, and face challenges with resilience. Neglecting mental health can lead to physical health issues and impact our daily functioning.

When should I seek help for my mental health? +
  • Emotional struggles last for more than two weeks
  • You find it difficult to manage daily activities
  • You have persistent feelings of hopelessness or anxiety
  • You experience thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Professional help, such as therapy or counseling, can provide tools and support to manage mental health challenges effectively.
